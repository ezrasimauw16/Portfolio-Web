INFORMATICS STUDENT · UNSRAT

Ezra Steve Simauw.

I have always been an artist by nature. Long before I knew what a line of code was, my world was defined by the scratch of a pencil on paper and the melodies I found on the piano and guitar. As a native of Manado currently studying Informatics at UNSRAT, I'll be the first to admit that my path hasn't been a straight line — I chose the future, but I never lost the passion.

That choice made me a "flexible" creator in every sense of the word. I am a singer, a dancer, and a musician; a photographer, a graphic designer, and a video editor. My musical influences — from the timeless precision of Michael Jackson and the Jackson 5 to the nostalgic harmonies of the Backstreet Boys — infuse my visual work with rhythm and energy. I don't just see a design or a photograph; I feel the tempo and the groove of the composition.

To me, Informatics provides the skeleton, and art provides the soul. My technical studies taught me a level of logic and meticulousness that helps me design digital experiences that aren't just pretty, but functional. I refuse to be labeled as "just" a student or "just" an artist. I am a multidisciplinary creator who believes that logic and creativity are two sides of the same coin.
